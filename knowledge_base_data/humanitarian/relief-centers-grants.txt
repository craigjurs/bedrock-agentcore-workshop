HOW TO APPLY FOR AID GRANTS

Step 1: Determine Eligibility
- Must demonstrate financial need
- Must have experienced qualifying emergency or disaster
- Must provide required documentation
- Must be resident of service area

Step 2: Gather Required Documents
- Proof of identity (government-issued ID)
- Proof of income (pay stubs, tax returns, benefit statements)
- Proof of emergency/disaster (photos, reports, insurance claims)
- Proof of expenses (bills, receipts, estimates)
- Bank statements (last 3 months)

Step 3: Submit Application
Online Application:
- Visit redcross.org/apply-for-aid
- Create account or log in
- Complete application form
- Upload required documents
- Submit application
- Receive confirmation email with application ID

In-Person Application:
- Visit nearest Red Cross Relief Center
- Speak with case manager
- Complete paper application
- Submit documents in person
- Receive application receipt with ID

Phone Application:
- Call 1-800-RED-CROSS (1-800-733-2767)
- Speak with intake specialist
- Complete application over phone
- Mail or email required documents
- Receive confirmation with application ID

Step 4: Application Review Process
- Initial review: 3-5 business days
- Additional documentation may be requested
- Case manager assigned for complex cases
- Decision typically within 10-15 business days
- Notification via email, phone, or mail

Step 5: Grant Disbursement
- Approved grants disbursed within 5-7 business days
- Payment methods: Direct deposit, check, gift cards, direct payment to vendor
- Disbursement method depends on grant type

GRANT APPLICATION STATUS CHECK

To check your application status:
1. Visit redcross.org/application-status
2. Enter your Application ID (format: GR-YYYY-XXXXXX)
3. Enter your email address or phone number
4. View current status and next steps

Or call your assigned case manager using the contact information provided in your confirmation email.

AVAILABLE GRANT TYPES

Emergency Housing Assistance:
- Temporary housing costs
- Security deposits
- First month's rent
- Maximum: $3,000 per application

Disaster Relief:
- Property damage repairs
- Temporary lodging
- Essential items replacement
- Maximum: $5,000 per disaster event

Emergency Food Assistance:
- Grocery assistance
- Meal programs
- Maximum: $500 per application

Medical Emergency Assistance:
- Medical bills
- Prescription costs
- Medical equipment
- Maximum: $2,000 per application

Utility Assistance:
- Electric, gas, water bills
- Prevent service disconnection
- Maximum: $1,000 per application

Emergency Transportation:
- Vehicle repairs
- Public transit assistance
- Maximum: $750 per application
